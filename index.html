Why a Cornell Tech MBA?

To learn the art of leadership, excel as a product manager, and grow my passion for innovating with technology to create value and impact.

Create and Innovate

I love to learn, and I particularly enjoy learning by doing. At Cornell Tech, I would have the freedom to experiment with new ideas, build innovative technology products, and experience repeated iterations of the product cycle. In doing so, I will gain valuable leadership and product experience, while also building something I can be proud of and— possibly even—take to market.

Through its studio environment and its unique curriculum including the Startup Project, Cornell Tech is the ideal place for the experiential learning that I seek.

Focus on Product Experience

I hope to gain more core product management experience and focus on driving product strategy and development in an established business environment. Whether it's a consumer or enterprise product, software or hardware, I hope to gain experience with a broad spectrum of technology products. Meanwhile, I am also actively improving my technical skills, deepening my expertise in web development and system architecture.

I am particularly thrilled with Cornell Tech’s Company Projects. This experience will give me the opportunity to apply myself and explore innovative solutions to challenging issues in an environment with a very mature technology stack.

Network

The early stage start-up environment excites me like no other. People get together, share ideas, and challenge each other as they bring their product to life. It is this experience that I hope to share alongside the talented students and faculty of Cornell Tech. Particularly, I am impressied with the faculty and researchers at Cornell Tech who have such rich, real-world success. It would be a great priviledge to study and grow among this amazing network of people.

Store Maintenance App

Helping store employees manage store maintenance activities at a retail chain of over 4000 stores nationwide

Issue: While juggling store operations, customer service, and endless other high priorities, store managers needed quick and easy visibility of maintenance activities occuring day-to-day in their store.

Impact: I led a team to design and build an iPad application that would provide this much-needed information, real-time, and at their fingertips. If they encounter a broken oven or a refrigeration alarm, managers can immediately put in a maintenance order with detailed notes and use their tablet-enabled camera for photos.

Technical environment: HTML5, Angular.js, Jira, SAP UI5

  • Date: September 2014
  • Client: Large multi-national retailer
  • Category: Product Management

Marketing Dashboard

Providing the Chief Marketing Officer with a pulse of current and upcoming marketing campaigns

Issue: With over dozens of national marketing campaigns occuring over many media (print, email, web, social media, and television), marketing executives including the Chief Marketing Officer need a way to visually track campaigns and their effectiveness

Impact: With this iPad application we sought to provide a high-level snapshot of our client's marketing campaigns and focus on clarity and understanding, while also providing enough detail to demonstrate campaign effectiveness. Given the complexity of campaigns and their varying key metrics, this application relied heavily on a clear and intuitive user interface. Included in this app were various integrations with external sources including social media (via Netbase) and news sources (via RSS feeds).

Technical Environment: HTML5, Backbone.js, Jira, SAP CRM, SAP NetBase

  • Date: March 2014
  • Client: Major telecommunications provider
  • Category: Product Management

Sales Rep iPad App

An outside salespersons’ personal assistant and companion helping them at every step of the sales process

Issue: Outside sales representatives spend much of their work day on the road visiting their customers, following up on shipments, and selling more product. Pulling out a laptop during a customer visit to complete an order seemed to alienate the customer interaction.

Impact: Our goal was to build a mobile application that would provide sales representatives the right information at the right time, whether it was in their car looking for directions to their next customer or sitting down with the customer discussing the latest products. This app covered all their needs from route planning and customer relationship management to product catalogs and order processing.

Design Tools: Axure, Balsamiq, Sketch, Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, Photoshop, Fireworks)

  • Date: January 2014
  • Client: Deloitte Consulting Innovation
  • Category: UX Design

Purchasing Catalog App

Using intelligent design to achieve business objectives of increasing profit by maximizing inventory turnover

Issue: As a large product distributor, this client had significant excess inventory primarily because purchasing agents were not effectively informed of inventory levels prior to making purchasing decisions.

Impact: Beyond simply designing a functional application, we wanted it to provide thoughtful insights to help drive purchasing decisions such as what to buy and –just as importantly– what not to buy. The home page was designed to be simple and feature discounted items as well as bring attention to slow-moving products, allowing agents to curate a shopping cart that closely matched customer demand, thereby maximizing sales and reducing inventory levels.

Design Tools: Axure, Balsamiq, Sketch, Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, Photoshop, Fireworks)

  • Date: May 2014
  • Client: Large US beverage distributor
  • Category: UX Design

Plant Maintenance Transactions

Simplifying the user experience of SAP for plant maintenance technicians

Issue: Traditionally, SAP is known to have a very unfriendly user interface. And given that plant maintenance technicians experience high turnover rates and typically lack technical experience, our client needed a quick and inexpensive way to improve the user experience for its technicians.

Impact: I led a team to redesign their SAP plant maintenance screens. With just 5 weeks of user research and agile development, we were able to design, build, and test this redesign for a user base of over 10,000 technicians.

  • Date: July 2014
  • Client: Chemicals manufacturing company
  • Category: SAP Screen Personas

Gasha Microfinance

In today’s environment, you wouldn’t run a grocery store with an abacus. Neither should microfinance organizations run without enterprise software.

Issue: Gasha Microfinance is a small microfinance institution in Ethiopia that helped provide microloans to local entrepreneurs. Despite growing year over year for the last decade, Gasha has not yet achieved profitability. One of the primary causes is that loan officers could not scale their customer base effectively due to its paper-and-pencil based information system.

Impact: I spent a week in Ethiopia to help them assess the possible benefits of a management information system (MIS) in increasing efficiency in loan processing. In addition, I designed a MIS selection scorecard, allowing them to objectively assess MIS solutions in the future.

  • Date: November 2013
  • Client: Gasha Microfinance
  • Category: Tech Strategy
